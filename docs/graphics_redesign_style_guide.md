# Graphics Redesign Style Guide

Status: future plan

This document defines the intended visual direction for any future graphics redesign of Dead Pixel Survival. The goal is to keep the game original while preserving the readable, handcrafted feel of pixel art survival games.

## Core Direction

- Handcrafted pixel art with a moody survival tone.
- Readable at small sizes, with strong silhouettes and simple forms.
- Consistent pixel grid across sprites, UI, props, and environments.
- Decayed, lived-in world design rather than clean or cozy presentation.
- Warm interior light contrasted against cold outdoor scenes.

## Visual Principles

- Keep sprites chunky and immediately legible.
- Use a limited number of value steps per material.
- Favor hard pixel transitions over soft gradients.
- Use atmosphere through lighting, fog, rain, smoke, and color choice instead of heavy detail.
- Let environmental storytelling show the history of the world: barricades, burned areas, graves, abandoned vehicles, and repaired structures.

## Color Direction

- Primary tones: slate blue, moss green, charcoal, dirty beige, rust, and candlelight amber.
- Keep overall saturation low.
- Reserve brighter colors for gameplay-critical details such as warnings, health states, or interactive objects.
- Avoid glossy highlights and overly cheerful palettes.

## Character Art

- Keep character designs simple and expressive.
- Use clear outlines or strong silhouette contrast where needed.
- Slightly stylized proportions are acceptable, but avoid chibi cuteness and realistic anatomy.
- Animation should stay restrained and functional: idle sway, impact frames, breathing, and small reactive motions.

## Environment Art

- Prioritize believable survival spaces: boarded windows, cracked roads, abandoned houses, overgrown yards, ruined storefronts, and improvised defenses.
- Make every district visually distinct through shape language, props, and color accents.
- Use weather and time of day as major mood drivers.

## UI Art

- Keep UI utilitarian and readable.
- Use notebook, paper, survival gear, and worn signage cues instead of clinical panels.
- Icons should be simple and consistent across the whole interface.
- Preserve gameplay readability over decorative detail.

## Procedural or Code-Generated Art

- If art is generated by code later, keep the style original and modular.
- Use palette swaps, seeded variation, and modular parts rather than noise-heavy random detail.
- Preserve silhouette readability and pixel consistency.
- Do not let procedural variation break the game's visual identity.

## Avoid

- Photorealism.
- Painterly or blurry rendering.
- Heavy anti-aliasing that fights the pixel look.
- Overly cute farm-sim brightness.
- Excessive micro-detail that disappears in motion.
- Mixed-resolution assets or inconsistent pixel scaling.

## Future Use

This guide can be used later for:

- concept art prompts
- asset generation prompts
- procedural sprite generation rules
- UI redesign references
- environment and tile-set direction
